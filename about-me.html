About Edit and her project

Edit Olah, Hungarian-born, London-based artist, in her project 'Can you see yourself ?' attempts to generate an internal conversation about the effects that our past has on our present and future.

Her art works offer a glimpse into feelings generated by memories.

As Edit says:

'The project ‘Can you see yourself?’ came from a decision I made to try to understand my memories, my experiences and their effect on my life. I want to understand how the way we deal with the emotions rooting in our past transforms us and influences our present and future.

I hope that the pieces help the viewer to find and connect to their own experiences, to engage with their own story.

I would like the viewer to connect to the universal meaning and message that is present in the work. I would like the audience to find their own signs, prompting them to change whatever their situation and their future require.'

Edit's multimedia installations combine ancient materials and techniques - like wool, felt, copper and printing - with moving image accompanied by her own spoken word and singing.

In Edit's words:

'The circle of forms and media I am using has been expanding. I am exploring ways of combining photography, writing, graphics, animation, moving image, felting, sewing, sculpture and installation... and the list is growing.'
